Common Plumbing Issues

Low water pressure can occur due to clogged aerators, pipe leaks, faulty valves, mineral buildup, or issues with the main water supply. Our technicians can quickly diagnose and resolve the problem.

Common signs include unusually high water bills, damp walls, peeling paint, mold growth, or the sound of running water when fixtures are turned off. We can help identify and repair hidden leaks efficiently.

Avoid using harsh chemical cleaners, as they can damage pipes. Try using a plunger for minor clogs. If the blockage persists, contact us for professional drain cleaning services.

Discolored water may result from rust in aging pipes, sediment buildup, or temporary maintenance work by the water supplier. If the issue continues, we recommend a professional inspection.

Maintenance & Prevention

We recommend a plumbing inspection every 1–2 years to identify potential issues early, especially in older homes or properties with recurring plumbing problems.

Use drain strainers, avoid disposing of grease and food waste in sinks, and do not flush non-biodegradable items. Regular maintenance can help keep your drains flowing smoothly.

Most water heaters last around 8–12 years. If you notice leaks, inconsistent heating, unusual noises, or rising energy bills, it may be time for a repair or replacement assessment.
